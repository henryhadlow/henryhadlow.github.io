Government Digital Service – product manager

June 2015 – present

I’m currently working on GOV.UK Notify. We’re making it easy for government services to keep users up-to-date with text messages, emails and letters. For instance, if you send in your passport as part of your student loan application, we’ll text you to say we’ve got it. Currently millions of people call government to check up on this kind of thing – this costs them time, and government money.

Read our blog post – Introducing GOV.UK Notify

I also designed and currently manage petition.parliament.uk. In the first 6 months, the site has resulted in 17 debates in the House of Commons, on issues like refugees and immigration, junior doctors’ contracts, legalisation of cannabis and whether to ban Donald Trump from the UK. The site has handled over 7m signatures and been used by more than one in ten adults in the UK.

Hear an interview I did on BBC Radio 4 about our busiest day.

Read a short blog post about how we made it easier to sign petitions.

Government Digital Service – lead designer

August 2012 – June 2015

I worked on the launch of GOV.UK. We created one place for all government services and information, won Design of the Year 2013 and a D&AD Black Pencil. GOV.UK costs £100m less each year than the websites it replaced, and 300 departments and agencies now have web pages that work the same way.

Watch a video I made briefing GOV.UK to the Cabinet

I also worked to simplify applying for a Lasting Power of Attorney. Previously, most people paid a lawyer £1,000+ to prepare their LPA, and even so, around 20% of LPAs had legal errors that made them unusable. The process is much simpler now. Most people make their LPA themselves, and even without a lawyers, only 1% of LPAs have legal errors. This is because I looked exhaustively at all the errors people make and designed them out.

s[edition] – lead designer

August 2011 – May 2012

Artists such as Damien Hirst and Tracey Emin typically sell limited edition works in small numbers, for large sums money. s[edition] flips that model on it’s head. Works are sold in much larger editions but for small sums, making art affordable for everyone.

We worked with leading artists including Damien Hirst and Tracey Emin, as well as digital art heavyweights including Rafael Rozendaal and Universal Everything.

Working with renowned graphic designer Jonathan Barnbrook, I created a visual identity that worked for art-world and digital audiences. Whilst other startups were still doing web-2.0-style designs, we were forging ahead with a simple, modern look designed to bring the artworks to the fore.

The Partners – designer

July 2010 – August 2011

I worked for a large insulin brand, producing materials explaining how and when to prescribe the insulin. I was briefed to produce a book for employees to help them navigate an enormous library of material: scientific studies, literature reviews and video tutorials for patients. I suggested an app – it automatically stays up-to-date, and you can email material to doctors right from within the app. The client loved it and it got The Partners loads more work.

Take a look on my old portfolio site

I also worked pro-bono on a fundraising campaign for Richard House Children’s Hospice. We asked over a hundred professional illustrators to play a giant game of consequences, then created a website where you can mix and match their illustrations to create your own combination, which you can buy, as long as nobody else has beaten you to it!
