Recent work

petition.​parliament.​uk – tell the government what you think

We’re giving citizens a voice. If your petition gets 10,000 signatures, the government will respond. And at 100,000 signatures, your petition will be considered for a debate in the House of Commons.

In just 6 weeks I overhauled the old ‘e-petitions’ site to make it simpler, clearer and faster. Signing a petition now involves entering just three pieces of information. A new email confirmation pattern means 50,000 more people use the service successfully every month.

www.gov.uk/performance – performance data for government services

Government provides over 800 services – things like renewing a passport or booking a driving test. We’re using data to help improve them.

When I joined the team they were celebrating the release of the 10th dashboard. Now we have over 800, one for every service. I’ve pushed for a simpler, clearer codebase and faster dashboard creation – it’s now much faster to iterate and improve the product.

I also worked closely with our user researcher, Will Roissetter, to help the team understand the user needs of civil servants.

Lasting Power of Attorney – radically simplifying a complex legal process

Lasting power of attorney (LPA) is a powerful legal document lets you choose people you trust to make decisions for you.

I redesigned the LPA application process and the paper LPA forms. These are the best-researched forms in government, with 40+ hours of user research and many participants aged 70+.

On the old forms, about 20% of LPAs had mistakes that made them legally invalid. With the new forms, the error rate is less than 1%.

For the first time ever, the LPA call centre got so many calls giving positive feedback, they added a ‘positive feedback’ button to their call handling system. And the design patterns I established are being used across government.

www.seditionart.com – pioneering a new way to buy art

seditionart.com is a startup selling digital art in limited editions. We worked with leading artists including Damien Hirst and Tracey Emin, as well as future digital art heavyweights including Rafael Rozendaal and Universal Everything.

Working with reknowned graphic designer Jonathan Barnbrook, I created a visual identity that worked for art-world and digital audiences. Whilst other startups were still doing web-2.0-style designs, we were forging ahead with the simple, modern look designed to bring the artworks to the fore.
